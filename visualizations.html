What If All Vehicles Were Electric?

In today’s cities, increasing population and urbanization have become one of the main sources of environmental problems. Particularly in major cities like Istanbul, heavy vehicle traffic plays a critical role in the rise of carbon emissions and air pollution. This project aims to gain a deeper understanding of the environmental impact of the transportation sector. Our project will compare the annual carbon emissions of electric and conventional vehicles to evaluate what kind of transformation is needed for a more sustainable transportation model.

Descriptive Visualizations

The graph shows the annual carbon emissions for different fuel types. Gasoline vehicles have the highest emissions, while electric vehicles have the lowest emissions. Electric vehicles do not produce direct carbon dioxide emissions; the emissions result from the electricity generation process used to charge the vehicles.

We know that the indirect carbon dioxide emissions of electric vehicles are lower than the emissions from other vehicles' fuel consumption. The graph shows how carbon dioxide emissions would change over the years if all cars in traffic were electric. As a result, it has been observed that switching to electric vehicles can reduce carbon dioxide emissions by approximately 24% each year. Looking at the past 20 years, it is evident that transitioning to all-electric vehicles could significantly reduce emissions.

This graph is showing the predictions for future years vehicle number. The numbers of electric and non-electric vehicles were modeled using linear regression based on real data. The dashed lines represents the predictions for future years made using this model.

The number of non-electric vehicles continues to increase, which may worsen environmental problems. On the other hand, the rapid growth in the number of electric vehicles indicates an acceleration in the transition to clean energy. The increasing adoption of electric vehicles is critically important for reducing the environmental impacts of fossil-fueled vehicles.

Model-Based Visualization

This graph illustrates the changes in carbon emissions over the years according to vehicle fuel types. Darker colours represent higher emissions, while lighter colours indicate lower emissions.

The increase in the use of electric vehicles is a sign of the growing preference for environmentally friendly choices. However, it is notable that while the number of diesel vehicles with lower carbon emissions has decreased in recent years, the number of gasoline vehicles with the highest carbon emissions has increased. This highlights that despite the rise in environmentally conscious choices, the increase in the number of high carbon-emission vehicles continues to have a negative impact on the environment.

In conclusion, electric vehicles clearly demonstrate significant advantages in reducing carbon emissions compared to fossil fuel-powered vehicles. The increasing adoption of electric vehicles signifies a positive shift towards more environmentally friendly transportation options, although the rising number of high-emission gasoline vehicles remains a notable concern.

To achieve substantial reductions in carbon emissions, it is essential to gradually phase out high-emission vehicles and support the development of sustainable transportation infrastructure. Additionally, when electricity is generated from renewable energy sources, the indirect emissions associated with electric vehicles can also be significantly minimized. This transition will play a vital role in combating climate change and preserving environmental health for future generations.
